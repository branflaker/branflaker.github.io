## 20. Lethal Company

![Screenshot depicting horde of bug enemies in Lethal Company](/assets/img/lethalcompany.jpg){: width="400" .right }
While mildly scary, it makes for a perfect evening wind-down game. The highlight of the year was unexpectedly getting killed by the tentacle creature at the company building while turning in scrap.

{: .clear }

## 19. Scavengers Reign

![Screenshot of imagery from Scavengers Reign](/assets/img/scavengersreign.webp){: width="400" .right }
When I was a kid, I was enjoyed watching and observing animals—just seeing what they would do. This show stimulates that same feeling in some ways. What stood out most to me was the alien creatures and the obvious thought that the creators put into their biology.

{: .clear }

## 18. Parasite

![Kim Ki-taek working as the driver for the Park family, still from the movie Parasite](/assets/img/parasite.jpg){: width="400" .right }
I started the year with the desire to watch more movies. I didn't really stick to that plan, but this was one of the first ones I watched. I like going in blind, and I wasn't sure what the movie was about but I really enjoyed the mix of dark humor and the escalating tension in the film.

{: .clear }

## 17. Pokemon TCG Pocket

![Screenshot of immersive Pikachu ex from Pokemon TCG Pocket](/assets/img/ptcgpocket.webp){: width="400" .right }
There was a big furor about this game when it came out, even among the Marvel Snap crowd. Like regular Pokemon TCG, but with much faster games. I was lucky enough to get several immersive cards, like the Pikachu card shown here.

{: .clear }

## 16. Everything Everywhere All At Once

![Evelyn Wang, still from the movie Everything Everywhere All At Once](/assets/img/everythingeverywhereallatonce.jpg){: width="400" .right }
I had heard a little about this movie before I watched it. After hearing enough about it, I decided to watch it and it was a lot goofier and more fun than I expected. I started it over after watching it a third of the way through just so that I could get my wife to watch it. Ke Huy Quan did a great job playing different characters. While multiverses are a popular movie trend these days, this movie does it incredibly well.

{: .clear }

## 15. The Quacks of Quedlinburg

![Box art of The Quacks of Quedlinburg board game](/assets/img/quacksofquedlinburg.webp){: width="400" .right }
As inherently social as they are, when my board games appeal to my family, they tend to rank higher. My oldest daughter really enjoyed playing this game, and it certainly helped that she kept winning. It's a push-your-luck game where you are trying to fill up a cauldron as much as possible, and the ingredients you purchase help determine how far this goes.

{: .clear }

## 14. Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood

![Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood artwork](/assets/img/fullmetal-alchemist-brotherhood.jpg){: width="400" .right }
I loved the original Fullmetal Alchemist, and the beginning is both timeless and heartbreaking. The ending, unfortunately, and in particular the movie, was a bit of a disappointment that came out of left field, due to outpacing the manga source material. Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood fortunately came out much later and had a much better story.

{: .clear }

## 13. Potion Explosion

![Potion Explosion box art](/assets/img/potion-explosion.webp){: width="400" .right }
While I'm not an avid Candy Crush player, this is basically that "Match 3" style of game in physical form. It's one of those games that gets people's attention when they walk past the table. Quick to learn, but there's some additional depth in when and how to use the potions you create.

{: .clear }

## 12. Cocoon

![Cocoon screenshot](/assets/img/cocoon.jpg){: width="400" .right }
This is an adventure puzzle game that's tricky enough to make you stop and think but easy enough to still make you feel smart. You carry around orbs that contain worlds in which you can enter. It starts to get interesting when you're carrying orbs in and out through other orbs.

{: .clear }

## 11. Gris

![Gris screenshot](/assets/img/gris.jpg){: width="400" .right }
Gris is "every frame a painting" in video game form. I frequently found myself thinking that as I played through this game.  Short and sweet.

{: .clear }

## 10. College Football 25

![College Football 25 -- is back!](/assets/img/cfb-25.jpeg){: width="400" .right }
This game has its quirks and it will certainly be improved upon in the next release, but I am so glad it's back. It's been over a decade since a college football game and I played quite a bit of this after it was released. I wish there was a way to save and resume games after they've already been started, though.

{: .clear }

## 9. Wandering Towers

![Wandering Towers in mid-game](/assets/img/wandering-towers.jpg){: width="400" .right }
There appears to be a trend in all of the board games on this list this year; potions and wizardry. This is a near perfect board game. Right amount of length, easy to learn and play, and has great table presence.

{: .clear }

## 8. Stranger Things Season 4

![Eddie Munson shredding](/assets/img/stranger-things-4.webp){: width="400" .right }
Probably the best season of this show since the first. Joseph Quinn played the Eddie Munson character perfectly. My older brothers played D&D when I was a kid, and Eddie seems like he would fit in perfectly with their friends. Playing your own Metallica concert while being hunted/surrounded by demonic bats was the pinnacle of the vibes they were going for with this show.

{: .clear }

## 7. Deadpool & Wolverine

![Deadpool & Wolverine screenshot](/assets/img/deadpool-wolverine.webp){: width="400" .right }
It does what it says on the tin.  It's a Deadpool movie; does not take itself seriously and neither should you -- it's a lot of fun.

Normally, friendly fire would be frustrating.  But it makes things tactically interesting, or better, funny.  Best moment in this game -- probably getting crushed by an alien carrier that my friends (accidentally??!?) shot down.

{: .clear }

## 4. Puzzmo Cross|word

![Puzzmo Cross|Word](/assets/img/puzzmo-crossword.png){: width="400" .right }
{% raw %}
I was introduced to Puzzmo's daily Cross|word earlier this year. Most crossword puzzles feel dry and need to be publishable in a newspaper, Puzzmo feels like a modern take on the medium.  Clues use modern slang and topics and reference diverse perspectives. Every puzzle is a reflection of the creator, and when you finish each puzzle, you're treated to a note from the creator sharing the puzzle's inspiration, which makes the puzzle feel more like a personal connection than just a test.
{% endraw %}

## 1. Astro Bot

![Astro Bot posing with another bot](/assets/img/astro-bot.webp){: width="400" .right }
Got this game around Christmas. Played the heck out of it. One of the only games that take advantage of the adaptive triggers, but it feels great -- simulates the tension of a bow and arrow, resistance of clicking in a button, very fun. Presentation is beautiful, extremely polished, you can interact with everything.  Distilled joy.
